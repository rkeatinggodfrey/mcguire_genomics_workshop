Workshop Description
--------------------
This intensive, hands-on workshop introduces participants to core concepts and tools for genome assembly and annotation. It is a student-centered course combining theoretical instruction with live coding sessions and interactive activities. Over structured modules, students will learn the foundations of genome sequencing, explore bioinformatic tools for quality assessment and assembly, and gain practical experience in identifying contaminants and annotating genomes. Through interactive, student-centered activities, participants will build a comprehensive understanding of genome structure, assembly pipelines, and annotation strategies using real- world datasets. Participants will enter the workshop with varying levels of technical and biological expertise. Our goal is to ensure that all participants develop a comparable level of proficiency in the practical application of genomics tools. Students’ evaluation will focus on three key indicators of success: (i) coding skills, (ii) genomics knowledge, and (iii) practical application of tools and concepts.

Workshop objectives
-------------------
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. Define and explain key genome assembly- and sequencing-related concepts using appropriate
scientific vocabulary.
2. Navigate and utilize the UNIX command-line environment for genome data analysis.
3. Retrieve datasets from NCBI and perform initial quality control and preprocessing.
4. Assemble genomes and assess assembly quality with multiple tools.
5. Identify and remove potential contaminants from genomic datasets using visualization tools like Blobplot.
6. Understand the principles of genome annotation and apply basic annotation techniques to identify genome features. 
7. Analyze and interpret results from annotation pipelines, including identifying transposons and structural variations.
8. Collaborate in interactive exercises reinforcing theoretical concepts through creative problem-solving.
9. Apply the concepts learned in class to different scenarios.
10. Critically evaluate the use of genomic datasets to apply different concepts and analyses.
